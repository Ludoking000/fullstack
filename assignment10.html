Understanding Special Characters

Special characters in HTML are represented using HTML entities. Here are some examples:

  • & - represents the ampersand (&)
  • < - represents the less than sign (<)
  • > - represents the greater than sign (>)
  • © - represents the copyright symbol (©)
  • ® - represents the registered trademark symbol (®)
  • ™ - represents the trademark symbol (™)

Why Use HTML Entities?

HTML entities are used to ensure that special characters are displayed correctly in web browsers. For instance, if you type & directly in your HTML, it may not be rendered correctly. Instead, use &.
